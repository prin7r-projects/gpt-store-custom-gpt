# 08 — Marketing Strategy

## Positioning

> **For founder-operators and ops leaders** who already use ChatGPT as a working tool, **SeriousSequel** is **a productized-engagement service** that finishes the conversation a Store-listed GPT politely ended — with named operators, weekly checkpoints, and artifacts that survive the chat — **unlike** agency-style retainers that bill before they ship, **because** the GPT is the audition and the engagement is the work.

## Messaging hierarchy

### Tier 1 — Headline (the one sentence)

> "The GPT was the first sentence. Now let's finish the conversation."

Used: hero h1, X bio, all email signatures, deck cover.

### Tier 2 — Sub-claim (the next sentence)

> We publish productized custom GPTs in the OpenAI Store as a discovery layer. The same teams behind the GPTs run real engagements behind them: weekly checkpoints, named operators, and deliverables that survive past the chat window.

Used: hero supporting paragraph, founder content lede, podcast intro paragraph.

### Tier 3 — Proof claims (the things we point to)

1. **Named operators.** The "After" half of every transcript card is signed.
2. **Files-you-own.** Artifacts ship into the buyer's Notion / Drive / Slack.
3. **Crypto-first checkout.** USDT/USDC settles in minutes; no procurement form.
4. **Public Store GPT.** The audition is free, public, and durable.

Used: pillar cards, FAQ answers, founder-led content openings.

### Tier 4 — Anti-claims (what we explicitly are not)

- Not an "AI agency."
- Not a chat tool.
- Not a calendar booking funnel.
- Not a SaaS subscription with a dashboard.

Used: founder content for differentiation. Never used in the hero — anti-claims feel defensive when they lead.

## Content pillars

We rotate four content pillars across founder content, GPT publications, and community appearances. Every post fits one.

### Pillar 1 — How a specific Store GPT was built

Long-form essays under a real operator's name. ~1,200 words. Topic: "We built *Ops Pricing Architect* by running it against six real client engagements. Here's what made it ship."

**Distribution.** Site `/notes/`, X thread, ops-Slack mention.

**Cadence.** Once per fortnight.

### Pillar 2 — Specific failure modes of competing approaches

Short essays naming what didn't work in a recent engagement and how the GPT-then-engagement pattern handled it. ~800 words.

Topic example: "Why the agency RFP-decoder we hired in February shipped a 40-page report and missed the actual scope shift on page 12."

**Distribution.** Site `/notes/`, X thread.

**Cadence.** Once per month.

### Pillar 3 — Operator names with their work

Short founder posts attributing recent artifacts to their named operators. Builds operator credibility — and ours.

Topic example: "J. Romero shipped a Notion-page rate card for a 12-person research firm in 4 working days. Here's the structure."

**Distribution.** X thread, site footer rotation.

**Cadence.** Twice per month.

### Pillar 4 — Quiet receipts

Quotes from past engagements, dated, with one number. No marketing language; minimal context.

Example: "Renewed for month two. Tuesday checkpoint became a calendar fixture in their org. — A. Park, founder, Mar 2026."

**Distribution.** Landing page proof section, X bio rotation.

**Cadence.** Once per quarter.

## Channel-specific messaging

### OpenAI GPT Store description (each GPT)

Format: 1-line claim · 1-line scope · 1-line handoff line.

Example for *Ops Pricing Architect*:

> Pricing diagnostics for B2B services teams. Anchors price to outcomes, surfaces hidden churn risk in your last six engagements. When the conversation outgrows the chat window, the team behind it is at gpt-store-custom-gpt.prin7r.com.

### Founder X bio

> SeriousSequel / a Prin7r imprint that publishes productized GPTs as the first sentence of longer engagements. set in serif.

### LinkedIn (founder profile only)

Used minimally. One post per quarter. Always linking to a real essay, never a marketing page.

### Press / podcast positioning

Single-line: "We're SeriousSequel. We use OpenAI's GPT Store as a free, public audition for productized service engagements."

## Tone parameters

- **Sentence length.** 12–20 words median. We allow long sentences when the structure needs them.
- **Vocabulary.** Concrete nouns over category words. "BambooHR step" not "integration."
- **Pronouns.** "We" rare; "the operator" or a name preferred.
- **Adjectives.** Earned only. "Quiet" is allowed; "best-in-class" is forbidden.
- **Emoji.** None.
- **Numbers.** Always specific. "Four working days" not "fast."

## Brand assets in marketing

Every marketing surface — landing, content, X thread headers — uses:
- Source Serif 4 / Inter / JetBrains Mono trio
- Paper background
- One saffron highlighter mark per visible viewport
- No photography unless it is a real (single) operator portrait, greyscale + saffron duotone

## What we measure

- **Paid engagements per month.** The only acceptance criterion.
- **Tier mix.** Drift from the assumed 60/30/10 triggers a positioning review.
- **Time from Store GPT use → first invoice.** Median should track to ~7 days; if it grows past 21 days, the deep-link copy in the Store GPTs needs a rewrite.
- **Repeat-customer rate.** We accept moderate retention but track decline carefully.

We do not measure clicks, page-views, or "leads."

## Guardrails

- No A/B testing the hero copy. The headline is editorial, not statistical.
- No popup overlays.
- No chat widgets.
- No urgency phrases ("limited spots").
- No newsletter signup forms.
- No analytics beyond the host's standard access logs.
