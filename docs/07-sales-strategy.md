# 07 — Sales Strategy

## Motion

**PLG-led with a high-touch tail.**

The Store-listed GPT is the product-led discovery layer. Pricing is published; sign-up is replaced with a single payment action; engagement is high-touch only after the visitor has paid.

We do *not* do:
- Discovery calls
- SDR outreach
- Demo bookings
- Free trials with a credit card on file
- "Schedule a 15-minute chat with the founder"

We *do* do:
- Free, public Store-listed GPTs that produce real artifacts
- Published pricing
- Crypto-first checkout that clears in minutes
- Named operators who reply to a paid invoice within 24 hours

## Pricing tiers

### Tier 1 — Pay-per-engagement — $480 / engagement

**Who it's for.** Founders + ops generalists who want to test the team behind the GPT before any commitment.

**What's included.**
- 1 named operator, 1–2 working sessions, async between
- Single deliverable + 7-day fix window
- No retainer, no automatic renewal

**Why $480.** It's lower than a single discovery call from a competing agency. It's a number a founder can spend without consulting a co-founder.

**Margin.** Operator time at ~$120/hr loaded → 4 hours = $480. Break-even at delivery; profit on referral / upsell.

**Renewal logic.** ~30% of Pay-per-engagement customers convert to Subscription within 60 days.

### Tier 2 — Monthly subscription — $1,480 / month + $690 onboarding

**Who it's for.** Buyers who already trust the team (either via a previous Pay-per-engagement, peer referral, or strong Store GPT signal) and want continuity.

**What's included.**
- Private GPT trained on the buyer's context
- 2 named operators, Tuesday + Thursday checkpoints
- Editable workspace (Notion or buyer's stack)
- Cancel after any 30-day cycle

**Why $1,480.** Calibrated against three reference points: (1) a 200-person company can spend it under most ops directors' discretionary budget, (2) it's significantly less than a part-time fractional consultant ($4–8k/mo), (3) it produces 8–10 working artifacts per month — i.e., $150 per artifact.

**Margin.** ~32% gross. The cell of two operators consumes ~$1,000/mo of loaded time.

**Renewal logic.** Target 80% month-over-month retention. Cancellations are acceptable; we'd rather a customer leave cleanly than feel locked in.

### Tier 3 — Concierge engagement — $5,800 / month + $4,200 onboarding · 8-week minimum

**Who it's for.** Established companies (50–500 people) where one ops director is responsible for an outcome that needs three specialists (research, ops, comms) for two months.

**What's included.**
- 3-person delivery cell + named lead
- GPTs hosted under buyer's domain (paid OpenAI Team workspace)
- Single Slack/Telegram channel for work in flight
- 8-week minimum, then month-to-month

**Why $5,800.** Anchored to the cost of a single hire ($120k/yr → $10k/mo loaded) and discounted because we don't carry HR or benefits. Concierge is roughly 60% of the cost of a hire and ships in 8 weeks.

**Margin.** ~38%. Concierge is the highest-margin tier; it's also the highest-effort.

**Renewal logic.** Target 50% renewal at the 8-week mark. The Concierge tier exists partly to source long-term Subscription customers — many concierge engagements step down to Subscription.

## Objection handling

**"I want to talk to someone first."**
> Open one of the GPTs at the top of the page. Ask it the worst question you have. If the answer doesn't earn the next step, this isn't for you. — *We do not schedule calls. The GPT is the call.*

**"$480 is a lot for a single engagement."**
> The alternative is a $3k–$8k agency engagement that opens with a 4-week scoping phase. Our scoping phase is the GPT — and it's free.

**"What if I'm not satisfied?"**
> Pay-per-engagement comes with a 7-day fix window — we revise until the artifact lands. Subscription cancels at the end of any 30-day cycle. Concierge has an 8-week commitment, which is the shortest commitment a 3-person cell can run cleanly.

**"Can I pay by ACH / wire / card?"**
> NOWPayments hosted invoice supports a card on-ramp at the same URL. ACH/wire is available for invoices over $5k — email the desk.

**"Will my data be in some shared model?"**
> Subscription/Concierge GPTs run inside your own OpenAI Team workspace. We don't keep your data. The Notion docs we write live in your Notion.

**"How do I know your operators are real?"**
> Every operator signs the email they send. Their name appears in the engagement notes. References are gated to qualified Concierge inquiries.

**"What happens after the engagement?"**
> Files-you-own. Everything we deliver is in your stack from day one. There's no platform to leave; nothing to migrate.

## Sales rituals

- **Onboarding within 24 hours.** A NOWPayments invoice firing the IPN webhook triggers the operator-pairing protocol. The customer hears from a named human within one business day.
- **Tuesday/Thursday cadence (Subscription).** Every active subscription has a 30-min Tuesday review and a Thursday delivery. No exceptions.
- **End-of-week artifact (Concierge).** Every Friday, the lead operator posts a single artifact summary. No status reports.
- **30-day check-in (Subscription).** At the end of each cycle, the lead operator asks one question: "Are we delivering enough that the answer's obvious?" If the answer is anything other than yes, the customer can cancel cleanly.

## What we will refuse

We will refuse customers who:
- Want to white-label the GPT itself (different service line).
- Demand on-prem deployment.
- Need a vendor security questionnaire taking >2 hours to complete.
- Cannot pay any tier within 7 days of agreeing.
- Are themselves an AI consulting firm trying to subcontract us.

Refusing a customer kindly is part of the sales motion.
