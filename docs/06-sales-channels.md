# 06 — Sales Channels

## The mix

| Channel                      | Weight | Role                                               |
| ---                          | ---    | ---                                                |
| OpenAI GPT Store             | 60%    | Primary discovery + qualification                  |
| Peer referrals (organic)     | 18%    | Mid-funnel proof, esp. for Subscription tier       |
| Founder content (X / blog)   | 12%    | Top-of-funnel pull, brand reinforcement            |
| Slack / Discord communities  | 6%     | Targeted appearance in 4–5 ops/AI communities      |
| Direct outbound              | 3%     | Concierge tier only; named target list             |
| Press / podcast              | 1%     | Opportunistic; never planned                       |

## 1. OpenAI GPT Store (primary)

**Why it fits.** Our entire ICP is already in the GPT Store. Maya is there at 11pm. Daniel's peer is there during a 4-minute coffee break. Lin is there pasting a runbook prompt.

**How we win.** Each published GPT is built around a single, *unsolved* operational pain — pricing, RFP decoding, runbook drafting, research note triage, pipeline diagnostics. The GPT does a serious job on its narrow problem and politely ends with our deep link.

**Quality bar.** No GPT is published until a senior operator has stress-tested it against three real client engagements. We'd rather publish two excellent GPTs than ten mediocre ones.

**Distribution mechanics.** OpenAI's Store ranks GPTs by usage + retention + ratings. We don't game the ranking; we ship GPTs that *get used twice*. The second use is the whole game.

**Risk.** OpenAI changes Store policy, removes our GPTs, or de-platforms publishers. We mitigate by mirroring our GPTs as private OpenAI Team workspace deployments for current Subscription/Concierge clients — if the public Store goes away, paid clients are unaffected.

## 2. Peer referrals

**Why it fits.** Maya talks to other founders. Daniel is in an ops directors' Slack. Lin's CEO talks to other agency CEOs. Our target audiences are tightly clustered.

**How we win.** Every paid engagement closes with a polite, opt-in ask: "Anyone in your network we should publish a GPT for next?" We do not run a referral incentive program — the incentive is the GPT itself, which we will tune to their adjacent peer's exact problem.

**Mechanic.** Each referral that lands a paid engagement gets a $200 credit toward their next month, applied silently. We don't advertise the credit because it changes the conversation.

**Quality bar.** A referral only counts if it produces a paid engagement, not a tire-kicker.

## 3. Founder content (X / blog)

**Why it fits.** Maya scrolls X late at night. Daniel reads two newsletters weekly. Concise, opinionated writing about productized engagements does well in both.

**How we win.** One operator-led essay per fortnight, published under a real name. Topics: how a specific Store-listed GPT was built, what it doesn't do, and what the engagement behind it ships. No SEO posts. No "5 ways to..."

**Format.** ~1,200-word essays on a static section of `gpt-store-custom-gpt.prin7r.com/notes/` (planned for v0.2). Cross-post to X as a thread when the essay is genuinely shareable.

**Quality bar.** Each essay must contain at least one named client outcome with a number. No abstractions.

## 4. Slack / Discord communities

**Why it fits.** Daniel's ops directors community and Lin's agency-ops Discord are exactly where our ICP's peers cluster. Showing up consistently produces organic mentions.

**How we win.** One operator joins each community as themselves (not as a brand account). They answer questions; they share GPT links when relevant; they never spam. If asked for a recommendation, they give a careful answer that may or may not include us.

**Hard rule.** No drive-by promotion. The community is the product, not the funnel.

**Communities (initial four).**
1. RevOps Co-op (Slack, 4k members)
2. Ops Beyond Tools (Discord, 1.2k members)
3. Operators Guild (Slack, 2.5k members)
4. Founder Cabinet (Discord, 800 members)

## 5. Direct outbound (Concierge tier only)

**Why it fits.** Concierge engagements ($5,800/mo) need pre-qualified buyers. The Store will produce them but slowly.

**How we win.** A named target list of 50 organizations per quarter where the COO or Ops Director has a public footprint that suggests fit. One personal email per target, with a specific reference to their public work and a relevant Store GPT we built (or built for).

**Cadence.** No follow-up sequences. One email, one reply window, then close the loop. We do not exist to drip on people.

**Quality bar.** A 5–8% reply rate. If it drops below 3%, we re-think the list, not the cadence.

## 6. Press / podcast

**Why it fits.** Opportunistic only. A founder-led, productized AI service is a clean narrative for a few specific publications.

**How we win.** Reactive — if a journalist asks, we reply with one paragraph and a real client reference. No proactive press tours.

## What we don't do

- **Paid ads.** Performance ads don't ladder back to the kind of trust the Store-listed GPTs already produce. We'd be paying to surface above artifacts that are already winning organically.
- **Email lead-magnets.** No "Download our 10-page AI playbook." The published GPT is the lead magnet.
- **Webinar funnels.** No.
- **LinkedIn InMail / Sales Navigator outreach.** Outbound is hand-written, not platform-templated.
- **SDR teams.** We don't have one and won't hire one for this revenue line.

## Channel measurement

We measure each channel only on **paid engagements produced**, not impressions/clicks/lead form fills. The minimum unit of attention is a $480 invoice landing.

| Channel                  | Target paid engagements / month (steady state) |
| ---                      | ---                                            |
| GPT Store                | 18                                             |
| Peer referrals           | 5                                              |
| Founder content          | 3                                              |
| Slack/Discord            | 2                                              |
| Direct outbound          | 1 (Concierge bias)                             |
| Press / podcast          | <1 (opportunistic)                             |

Steady-state target: 30 paid engagements/month, blended across tiers.
