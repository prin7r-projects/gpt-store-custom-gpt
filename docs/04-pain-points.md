# 04 — Pain Points

The pains we solve are not abstract. They are concrete failure modes of the alternatives the visitor has already tried. Each pain is named, traced to its root cause, and tied to a competing alternative we replace.

## Pain 1 — "The chat ends but the problem doesn't."

**Symptom.** A founder uses ChatGPT or a Store-listed GPT for a real business question. The model answers competently within the chat, but the answer is generic, the context evaporates, and the user is left with the same problem plus 30 minutes lost.

**Root cause.** A chat interface is not a workspace. It has no persistence layer the user owns; no schedule; no sequencing across days; no artifact production beyond what fits in a conversation. The model's competence at *the question* doesn't translate to competence at *the engagement.*

**Competing alternative.** ChatGPT Plus / Pro standalone use.

**How we solve it.** We pick up exactly where the GPT politely stopped. Same diagnosis, real artifacts, weekly cadence. The visitor doesn't repeat the story; the operator opens with what the GPT already concluded.

---

## Pain 2 — "Agencies bill before they ship."

**Symptom.** Visitor has experienced (or watched a peer experience) an agency engagement that opened with a 4-week scoping phase, billed $30k–$80k, and closed with a slide deck no one read.

**Root cause.** Traditional agency economics depend on long sales cycles to amortize CAC. They charge for the audition because the audition costs them real money.

**Competing alternative.** Boutique consulting / agency retainers ($10k–$40k/month).

**How we solve it.** The GPT is the audition. By the time the visitor lands here, we've already proven we can answer their question. Pricing reflects the engagement, not the qualification. Pay-per-engagement at $480 is calibrated to be lower than a single discovery call from a competing agency.

---

## Pain 3 — "AI tools don't have a delivery side."

**Symptom.** Buyer signs up for an AI SaaS that promises to "do" a workflow (sales outreach, RFP review, onboarding ops). The tool produces output, but no one is responsible for *fitting* the output into the buyer's actual operations.

**Root cause.** Pure-software companies are unwilling to staff the human side because it breaks the SaaS gross-margin model. They sell tools and call the staffing problem "yours."

**Competing alternative.** AI-vertical SaaS ($500–$5,000/month for tooling alone).

**How we solve it.** Hybrid: the GPT is the tooling, the engagement is the staffing. Named operators who work in the buyer's tools (Notion / Slack / HubSpot) — not in a separate dashboard. The output lives in the buyer's stack from day one.

---

## Pain 4 — "I can't tell which AI consultant is real."

**Symptom.** Buyer searches "AI consulting" or "AI for B2B operations." Sees 4,000 LinkedIn profiles claiming the same five skills. Cannot distinguish between someone who has shipped and someone who has read.

**Root cause.** No public, low-stakes way to evaluate the consultant before paying for a discovery call. Resumés don't help — the field is too new for résumé-based filtering.

**Competing alternative.** LinkedIn-sourced freelance AI consultants.

**How we solve it.** The Store-listed GPT *is* the audition. It's free to use. If the GPT does a serious job answering a serious question, the visitor has already validated the team behind it. We replaced the cold sales call with a free, durable artifact.

---

## Pain 5 — "Procurement makes payment hard."

**Symptom.** Buyer wants to retain a small team for a one-month engagement, but their procurement process requires a 30-day net invoice, a vendor onboarding form, and a $5k minimum spend. The vendor is too small to pass.

**Root cause.** Enterprise AP systems are built for vendor relationships measured in years, not weeks. They are hostile to lightweight, fast-cycle engagements.

**Competing alternative.** Internal procurement workflows.

**How we solve it.** Crypto-first checkout via NOWPayments with USDT/USDC. Settlements clear in minutes. Card on-ramp available on the same hosted page for buyers who can expense personal cards. We never ask buyers to fill a vendor form to spend $480.

---

## Pain 6 — "Signing up creates lock-in I can't audit."

**Symptom.** Buyer signs up for a service, then discovers their data lives in the vendor's database, the vendor's GPT, the vendor's Notion. Cancellation = restart from zero.

**Root cause.** Vendors prefer lock-in. It boosts retention metrics.

**Competing alternative.** Most SaaS-style AI services.

**How we solve it.** Files-you-own as a value, not a courtesy. Every artifact lands in the buyer's Notion / Drive / Slack. We don't operate a "platform." Cancellation = stop the recurring invoice; nothing migrates.

---

## What we explicitly **do not** solve

- **Buyers who want a free everything.** The GPT itself is free. Engagements are not.
- **Buyers who want hands-off automation.** Our model is high-touch. If the buyer wants no humans, we are wrong for them.
- **Buyers who need on-prem deployment.** We work in the buyer's existing cloud workspaces (Notion, Slack, etc.). On-prem is out of scope.
